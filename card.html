Platypus

 

Did you know that the male platypus has a spur on the hind foot that delivers a venom capable of causing severe pain to humans?

  • Scientific Name: Ornithorhynchus anatinus
  • Type: Mammals
  • Diet: Carnivores
  • Size: Head and body, 38cm; tail, 12cm
  • Weight: 1.3 kg

The platypus is a semiaquatic egg-laying mammal endemic to eastern Australia, including Tasmania. Together with the four species of echidna, it is one of the five extant species of monotremes, the only mammals that lay eggs instead of giving birth to live young.
